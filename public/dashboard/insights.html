Executive snapshot across all three features

  • Kai dominates everything: 90% of volume (3,973 of 4,402) and 86% of spend ($66 of $77).
  • Cost split: Kai 86% · Goals 9% · AI Assets 5%.
  • Per-interaction cost is the inverse of volume: Goals $0.114 ≫ Kai $0.017 ≈ Assets $0.011 — Goals is the priciest per call (it ingests full page HTML + variation JS).
  • All three features are actively multilingual (English, French, German prompts/answers).
  • A lot of traffic comes from *.github.io demo/test pages (akmatoff, ivanpsarew2, artemuch27…) across both Assets and Goals → meaningful share is QA/internal testing, not just live customers.
  • Shared power user: account 12932 is the #1 AI-Assets user (79 traces) and a top Goals user (8) — a clear internal champion / heavy tester.
  • Real brands present: Graham&Brown, Eurowings, X-Bionic, Schmoove, Boulanger, Booking.com, Providence Health.

Product metrics — PM view activation · stickiness · retention · funnels

Reach (distinct actors)

  • AI Goals: 18 customers · 25 experiments · 26 sessions.
  • Kai: 3,514 sessions — no user/customer id in the export, so Kai is session-only (can’t do per-user metrics yet).

Active users & stickiness

  • AI Assets: avg 7.3 DAU, peak 14, 54 period-unique → stickiness (DAU/period) 0.13.
  • AI Goals: avg 3.4 DAU, peak 7, 18 unique → stickiness 0.19 (highest).
  • Kai: avg 160 active sessions/day, peak 1,839 → very high throughput, low repeat (0.05).

Engagement frequency

  • AI Assets: 6.9 traces/user (median 3, p90 13, max 79). Top-3 users = 43%, top-10% (6 users) = 57% of volume.
  • AI Goals: 3.2 traces/customer (median 2, max 11); top-3 = 47%.
  • Breadth: AI-Assets users touch ~2.3 distinct condition types each (max 11) — most stick to a couple of targeting modes.

Funnels & activation

  • AI Assets: prompt → asset 69% (256/371); 31% clarify. At session level 88% eventually produce an asset (clarify-then-succeed) → strong activation.
  • AI Goals: session → inspect HTML 100% → snippet generated just 24% (14/58). Big drop-off — only 1 in 4 sessions ships code.
  • Kai: 16% of conversations invoke a tool (doc lookup); 84% answered from context.

Session depth

  • AI Goals most iterative: 2.23 turns/session, 46% multi-turn (max 8).
  • AI Assets: 1.50 turns/session, 24% multi-turn.
  • Kai: 1.13 turns/session, only 5% multi-turn (max 37) — mostly one-shot, consistent with batch summaries.

Retention / weekly trend

  • AI Assets: W1 209 traces (25 users) → W2 162 (39 users) — volume dipped but active users grew 25→39 (broadening adoption). 37% of users return on a 2nd day.
  • AI Goals: W1 37 (14 users) → W2 21 (9) — declining; 44% return on a 2nd day.
  • Kai by week: 543 → 2,670 → 746 → 14 (boundary). W2 carries the Jun-10 batch.

When they use it (UTC)

  • AI Assets: 88% within 08–18h, peak 10:00, Mon–Thu heavy, near-zero weekends → classic EU business-hours B2B tool.
  • AI Goals: 91% business hours, peak 13:00, Tue-heavy.
  • Kai: only 36% in business hours, peak 00:00, Wednesday spike (2,098) → automated/batch, not human-paced.

AI Assets targeting agent371 traces · 280 assets · $4.14 · Jun 8–19 (11d)

Output & outcomes

  • Produced 280 assets: 171 segments + 109 triggers (≈1.6:1).
  • Outcome mix: segment-only 40% (148) · trigger-only 24% (88) · both 5% (20) · clarification 31% (114) · none <1% (1).
  • ~1 in 3 prompts ends in a clarifying question rather than an asset — the single biggest friction signal.
  • But when it does build an asset, it almost never needed to clarify first — cluster-level clarification rates are 0–3%. So clarifications are concentrated in “couldn’t act, asked instead” cases.
  • Only 22 traces generated more than one asset; most prompts → one asset.

Condition shape

  • Avg 2.1 conditions per asset; one outlier asset had 104 (a giant URL list).
  • 94% of conditions are “include”, only 6% exclude — users target in, rarely carve out.
  • Match types: CONTAINS ~74% (200) ≫ EXACT (30), GREATER (29), REGEX (18), EQUAL (8).
  • 27 distinct condition types in use — broad coverage of the targeting surface.

What people target

  • Top condition types: PAGE_URL 241, DEVICE_TYPE 134, GEOLOCATION 81, BROWSER 22, NEW_VISITORS 17, VISITS / LAST_VISIT 11 each.
  • Use-case clusters: Device 112 · Geography 78 · URL/page 65 · Visitor lifecycle 62 · Browser/OS/lang 34 · Custom data 20 · Timing/events 12.
  • Prompt shapes: device audience 113 · freeform 96 · short instruction 72 · geography 42 · lifecycle 20.
  • Context split: 224 from segment editor vs 142 from trigger editor.

Who & where

  • 54 users, 61 sites, 20+ domains — a real but concentrated base.
  • Heavy concentration: top user 12932 = 79 traces (21%); top-3 users = 43%; top site 32359 = 64.
  • 74 French prompts, 4 German, 31 with accented characters.
  • Busiest day Jun 9 (62 traces); active 12 days. Tool/entity lookups in just 11% of traces.
  • Efficiency: $0.011/trace but ~41k tokens/trace (large system prompt + reasoning).

AI Goals goals / Aria agent58 traces · $6.60 · Jun 9–19 (10d)

  • 24% (14/58) ended in a generated tracking snippet; the rest were answers / page inspection without final code.
  • 102 HTML inspections via inspect_html_code — avg 1.8 per session, up to 5 — the agent looks at the DOM repeatedly before committing.
  • Most expensive feature per call: $0.114/trace (~10× AI Assets) — it processes full page HTML + applied variation JS.
  • 4.9M tokens over 58 sessions.
  • 18 distinct customers, 25 experiments, 15 domains. Top domains: booking.com (11), github.io demos, kameleoon.com, google.com, boulanger.com, schmoove.fr.
  • Top customers: 10398 (11), 10117 (8), 12932 (8 — same power user as Assets).
  • All sessions run under agent goals on electra-2.0.0.
  • Busiest day Jun 9 (14); active 9 days. Smallest sample of the three — early adoption.

Kai experiment copilot3,973 traces · $66.16 · rolling 21 days

Two distinct workloads

  • Experiment summaries: 3,073 (77%) on gpt-4o-mini — auto-generated Insights/Recommendations.
  • Documentation Q&A / chat: 900 (23%) on gpt-4o. Model choice maps exactly to use case.
  • Massive spike Jun 10: 1,854 traces (~47% of the 21-day volume) in a single day — looks like a bulk/batch summary run, not organic chat.
  • ~189 traces/day average otherwise.

Behavior & cost

  • Recommendation tone (approx scan of 3,087 summaries): iterate 875 ≫ abort 454 ≫ ship 175 — “ship it” is rarest (~6%); most experiments are not clear wins.
  • 1,067 doc lookups across 632 traces (16% use tools) — mix of filesystem reads of .mdx docs and semantic queries (“simulate a test”, “custom data”, “différence entre A/B test et personnalisation”).
  • Languages: EN 2,405 · DE 387 · FR 281.
  • 86% of all AI spend. $0.017/trace, ~7.6k tokens/trace — cheap per call, expensive in aggregate via volume.

Caveats read the numbers with these in mind

  • Different windows. Kai is a rolling 21-day slice of a 5.3 GB export; AI Assets & Goals are full exports over ~11 / ~10 days. Volume comparisons are window-adjusted, not apples-to-apples.
  • Heuristic counts. Recommendation tone, language tallies, and French-prompt counts come from keyword/marker scans — directional, not exact.
  • Test traffic mixed in. *.github.io demo domains and the dominance of a couple of internal accounts mean a chunk of “usage” is QA/dogfooding.
  • Clarifications excluded from clusters. Clarification-outcome AI-Assets traces produce no asset, so they don’t appear in use-case cluster counts.
